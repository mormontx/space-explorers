Alien Architects: Designing Life for Exoplanets

Year 8 Science Activity Worksheet

Mission Briefing: Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our own solar system. Just like Earth, every exoplanet has a unique formation, structure, and temperature. As an astrobiologist, your mission is to analyze three real exoplanets and design a theoretical life form that could thrive in each extreme environment!

Kepler-10b: The Lava World

Planet Profile

Structure: Scorching Super-Earth
Atmosphere: Vaporized rock and silicate clouds
Surface Temperature: 1,500°C

This world is so hot that the surface is completely covered in oceans of molten lava. Water cannot exist here, and carbon-based life would instantly burn up.

Your Task: Think about a creature that could survive in a lava ocean. If it can't be made of carbon, could it be made of heat-resistant silicon? How does it move through thick magma? Describe its physical adaptations and what it might consume for energy.

Gliese 436 b: The Ice Giant

Planet Profile

Structure: Ice Giant
Atmosphere: Hydrogen, Helium, and Methane
Surface Temperature: -180°C

Water on this planet is frozen rock-solid, but other chemicals like methane might exist as freezing liquids. The atmosphere is extremely thick, creating crushing pressure.

Your Task: Design a life form that can withstand freezing temperatures and high pressure. Does it have thick insulation, or does it produce its own internal heat? Instead of breathing oxygen and drinking water, how might it use the abundant methane? Describe its survival strategy.

Proxima b: The Habitable Neighbor

Planet Profile

Structure: Terrestrial / Rocky Super-Earth
Atmosphere: Potentially Nitrogen and Oxygen
Surface Temperature: -39°C to 20°C

Proxima b sits perfectly in its star's "Habitable Zone," meaning liquid water could pool on its surface. It is very similar to Earth, but it orbits much closer to a dim red dwarf star.

Your Task: Since this planet is a "Super-Earth," it has stronger gravity than our planet. How would higher gravity affect the height and bone structure of the animals living there? How might plants or eyes adapt to the dim red light of its sun? Describe your creature's adaptations.
