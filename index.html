// THE BASICS

WHAT IS SPACE?

Before we dive deep, let's get our definitions straight.

STAR

A giant ball of superhot gas (plasma) held together by its own gravity. It is so massive that the pressure in its core causes nuclear fusion, which releases massive amounts of energy and light. Our Sun is a star!

PLANET

A large celestial body that orbits a star. Unlike stars, planets don't produce their own light (they reflect the star's light). They have cleared their orbit of other debris and have enough gravity to be round.

GALAXY

A massive, gravitationally bound system that contains stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter. Our solar system is just one tiny speck inside the Milky Way galaxy, which contains billions of stars!

// THE FIRST BIG QUESTION

WHY IS SPACE DARK?

[SUN]

OLBERS' PARADOX

If the universe is infinite and full of stars, why isn't the night sky blazing with light? This puzzle baffled scientists for centuries!

The Answer: The universe isn't infinitely old. Light from the most distant stars hasn't had time to reach us yet -- the universe is only 13.8 billion years old.
[RED]

REDSHIFT & EXPANSION

The universe is expanding! As space stretches, the light from distant stars gets stretched too -- shifting from visible light to invisible infrared and microwave radiation.

Fun Fact: The leftover glow from the Big Bang, called the Cosmic Microwave Background, fills all of space -- we just can't see it with our eyes!

// BUILDING WORLDS

HOW PLANETS FORM

From swirling dust to entire worlds -- click to add matter and watch planets grow!

Planets form from a protoplanetary disk -- a swirling cloud of gas and dust around a young star. Tiny particles collide and stick together, growing into planetesimals and eventually full planets! Click "ADD MATTER" to see the process in action.

[ROCKY]

ROCKY PLANETS

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars -- formed close to the Sun where it's too hot for gas to condense. Made of rock and metal.

[GAS]

GAS GIANTS

Jupiter and Saturn -- formed farther out where it's cold enough for gas to accumulate. Mostly hydrogen and helium!

[ICE]

ICE GIANTS

Uranus and Neptune -- the most distant, made of ices (water, ammonia, methane) with thick atmospheres.

// COSMIC STRUCTURES

TYPES OF GALAXIES

Galaxies are vast collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter bound together by gravity

SPIRAL

Beautiful rotating arms of stars and gas. Our Milky Way is a spiral galaxy!

Contains: 100-400 billion stars

ELLIPTICAL

Smooth, oval-shaped collections of old stars. The largest galaxies in the universe are elliptical!

Can contain: up to 100 trillion stars

IRREGULAR

No defined shape -- often formed by galaxy collisions. Chaotic but full of new star formation!

Examples: Large & Small Magellanic Clouds
[HOME]

OUR HOME: THE MILKY WAY

We live in a barred spiral galaxy about 100,000 light-years across. Our Solar System sits on a minor arm called the Orion Arm, about 26,000 light-years from the galactic center. It takes our Sun about 230 million years to orbit the center once -- that's called a galactic year!

// KNOW THE DIFFERENCE

METEOR vs METEORITE vs ASTEROID vs COMET

They all sound similar, but they're very different! Let's sort them out.

METEOR

"Shooting Star"

A streak of light in the sky when a small piece of space debris burns up in Earth's atmosphere. They're not actually stars!

Location Earth's Atmosphere
Speed 11-72 km/s
Size Grain of sand to pebble

METEORITE

"The Survivor"

A piece of space rock that survives the journey through Earth's atmosphere and lands on the ground. You can actually find and hold them!

Location Earth's Surface
Types Stony, Iron, Stony-iron
Fun Fact ~48 tonnes fall daily!

ASTEROID

"Rocky Wanderer"

A rocky or metallic body orbiting the Sun, mostly found in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter. Leftovers from the early solar system!

Location Mostly Asteroid Belt
Size Range 1m to 530km (Vesta)
Count 1+ million known

COMET

"Dirty Snowball"

A frozen ball of ice, dust, and gas that develops spectacular tails when it gets close to the Sun. The tail always points away from the Sun!

Location Oort Cloud / Kuiper Belt
Tail Length Up to 150 million km!
Famous Halley's Comet (76 yrs)

// THE MOST EXTREME OBJECTS

BLACK HOLES

Where gravity is so strong that nothing -- not even light -- can escape!

[ ACCRETION DISK & TIME DILATION ]
[ GRAVITATIONAL LENSING & EVENT HORIZON ]

A black hole forms when a massive star collapses at the end of its life. The gravity is so intense that it warps space and time around it. The boundary around a black hole is called the event horizon -- once you cross it, there's no coming back!

[STELLAR]

STELLAR BLACK HOLES

Formed from collapsed massive stars. About 5-100 times the mass of our Sun, compressed into a tiny space.

[SUPER]

SUPERMASSIVE BLACK HOLES

Millions to billions of solar masses! One lives at the center of nearly every galaxy -- including ours: Sagittarius A*

[TIME]

TIME DILATION

Near a black hole, time itself slows down! If you watched someone fall in, you'd see them freeze at the event horizon.

Could our galaxy be inside a black hole?

[CLICK TO REVEAL]

Some physicists have proposed this! The idea is that every black hole might contain a new universe inside it. Our observable universe could theoretically exist inside a black hole in a "parent universe." It's called cosmological natural selection -- but it's still highly speculative!

[?]

Can sound travel in space?

[CLICK TO REVEAL]

No! Sound needs a medium (like air or water) to travel through. Space is a near-perfect vacuum. However, NASA did "sonify" a black hole in 2022 -- they converted pressure waves in hot gas around a black hole into audible sound. It was a deep, eerie hum in B-flat, 57 octaves below middle C!

[?]

How many stars are there in the universe?

[CLICK TO REVEAL]

About 200 sextillion -- that's 200,000,000,000,000,000,000,000! That's more stars than grains of sand on every beach on Earth. And each one could have its own planets. The numbers are truly mind-boggling!

[?]

What happens if you fall into a black hole?

[CLICK TO REVEAL]

You'd experience "spaghettification" -- the gravity at your feet would be so much stronger than at your head that you'd be stretched into a long, thin strand like spaghetti! For a supermassive black hole, you might actually survive crossing the event horizon initially, but you'd never escape.

[?]

What is dark matter?

[CLICK TO REVEAL]

About 85% of all matter in the universe is "dark matter" -- we can't see it, touch it, or detect it directly! We only know it exists because of its gravitational effects on galaxies. It's one of the biggest unsolved mysteries in science. Normal matter (everything you can see) is only about 5% of the universe!

[?]

Does it rain diamonds on other planets?

[CLICK TO REVEAL]

Yes! On Neptune and Uranus, the extreme pressure and temperature can crush carbon atoms into diamonds. Scientists believe it literally rains diamonds deep in these planets' atmospheres! These diamonds could be millions of carats in size.
