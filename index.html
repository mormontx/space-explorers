The Atmospheric Journey

These terms describe the same object at different points in its path. A Meteoroid is a small rock floating in space. When it enters a planet's atmosphere and vaporizes, creating a flash of light, it is a Meteor. If it manages to survive the atmospheric friction and strikes the ground, it is called a Meteorite.

Gravitational Collapse

A star forms when massive clouds of cold molecular gas and interstellar dust undergo gravitational collapse. When the density and temperature reach extreme levels, nuclear fusion ignites.

1. Cloud
2. Protostar

Planetary Accretion

Planets coalesce from the leftovers of star formation. Electrostatic forces make tiny dust grains stick together, building up planetesimals. Eventually, gravity takes over, sweeping the orbit clean.

1. Nebula Disk
2. Accretion
3. Orbit Clear
Astro Physics Info: Heavy elements (silicates, iron) accrete close to the star forming rocky planets, while lighter volatiles (water, methane) freeze out further away, creating gas/ice giants.

Giga-Tardigrade

Flattened, segmented body with skeletal hydraulic legs to distribute massive gravitational loads. Utilizes silicon-carbon polymers to prevent cellular collapse under high pressure.

Adaptation Markers:
